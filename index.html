The 90-Minute Rule

Human sleep consists of cycles roughly 90 minutes long. Waking up at the end of a cycle, rather than in the middle of deep sleep, significantly reduces sleep inertia—that heavy, groggy feeling in the morning. Clinical data suggests most adults function best on 5 or 6 complete cycles.
